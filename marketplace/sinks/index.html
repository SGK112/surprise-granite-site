Frequently Asked Questions About Sinks

What is the difference between a farmhouse and undermount sink?
A farmhouse (apron-front) sink has an exposed front panel that extends past the countertop edge, creating a statement look. An undermount sink is installed beneath the counter for a seamless, easy-to-clean surface. Farmhouse sinks require a special cabinet cutout, while undermount sinks work with any compatible countertop.

What sink material is most durable?
Stainless steel (16-18 gauge) is the most popular and durable option, resistant to heat and stains. Fireclay is extremely durable and scratch-resistant, ideal for farmhouse sinks. What size sink do I need for my kitchen?
Standard kitchen sinks are 33 inches wide for a 36-inch base cabinet. Single bowl sinks range from 23-36 inches. For base cabinets 30 inches or smaller, choose a sink 27 inches or less. Measure your cabinet interior width and subtract 2-3 inches for proper fit. Depth typically ranges from 8-10 inches.
