Frequently Asked Questions About Faucets

What faucet finishes are most popular for kitchens?
Brushed nickel and matte black are the most popular kitchen faucet finishes. Brushed nickel hides fingerprints and water spots while complementing most countertop colors. Matte black creates a modern contrast. Brushed gold is trending for luxury kitchens, and chrome remains a classic, budget-friendly choice.
What is the difference between a pull-down and pull-out faucet?
A pull-down faucet has a spray head that pulls straight down into the sink, ideal for deep sinks and tall pots. A pull-out faucet has a spray head that pulls toward you, better for shallow sinks or when you need to fill containers outside the sink. Pull-down models are more popular and offer better water coverage.
Are KIBI faucets water efficient?
Yes! KIBI faucets meet WaterSense standards with flow rates of 1.8 GPM or less for kitchen faucets and 1.2 GPM for bathroom faucets. This saves up to 30% water compared to standard faucets without sacrificing performance. All KIBI faucets include ceramic disc cartridges for drip-free operation.
How much does faucet installation cost?
Basic faucet installation in the Phoenix area ranges from $150-$300. Most single-hole kitchen faucets are straightforward to install. Widespread bathroom faucets may cost more due to additional connections. Surprise Granite often includes faucet installation with countertop and sink projects for the best value.
Which faucet finish is best for hard water in Arizona?
Brushed nickel and brushed gold finishes are best for Arizona's hard water because they hide mineral deposits and water spots. Avoid polished chrome in high-use areas as it shows every spot. Matte black also performs well but may show white calcium buildup. Consider a water softener for the best results with any finish.
