Common Questions

Scottsdale Countertop FAQs

Why hire a Surprise contractor for Scottsdale counters?
Two reasons: pricing and self-performance. Scottsdale-based contractors price in their Scottsdale overhead. We don't, and we still bring the same quality and slab-yard relationships. On scope coordination — we self-perform countertop fabrication, cabinet install, and the cabinet-to-counter interface; you don't pay 3 separate trades to coordinate themselves.
Do you slab-select at the Scottsdale yards?
Yes. We meet you at Arizona Tile (Scottsdale yard, Tempe yard, or Phoenix), MSI Surfaces, Cosentino City Phoenix, or Sun Stone — wherever your selection is — and mark the specific slab(s) for your project. We document with photos and yard tags.
Can you handle Old Town historic district approvals?
Old Town Scottsdale and parts of Arcadia have historic / overlay-district review. We provide documentation to City of Scottsdale historic preservation in their required format. Lead time 3–6 weeks for review.
How do you price exotic slabs (Cristallo, Calacatta Borghini)?
Exotic and ultra-premium slabs are quoted by the slab, not by the square foot. We price the slab(s) at supplier cost + flat fabrication fee + install. Transparent line-item pricing — no markup percentage games.
Mitered edges on a Calacatta marble waterfall?
Yes. Mitered-edge waterfalls in marble require precise fabrication — the diagonal cut shows the slab's full thickness profile. We do this routinely. Lead time 10–14 days fabrication, vs 7–10 for standard counter.
Large-format Dekton or Neolith — single-slab kitchens?
Yes. We've installed full Dekton and Neolith kitchens with single-piece islands up to 12 ft. These large-format porcelain slabs require two-person delivery, A-frame transport, and careful cutout work; we have the equipment.
