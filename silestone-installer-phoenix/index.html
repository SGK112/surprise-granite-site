Common Questions

Silestone FAQs

Is Surprise Granite a Silestone installer?
Yes. We install the full Cosentino line — Silestone (engineered quartz), Dekton (ultra-compact surface), and Sensa (sealed natural granite) — across the Phoenix metro. We fabricate in our in-house CNC shop in Surprise, AZ. Free in-home estimate, free sample by mail, typical timeline 7–10 days from slab selection to installation.
How much does Silestone cost in Phoenix?
Silestone installed in the Phoenix metro typically runs $55–$120 per square foot. Average kitchen total: $5,000–$9,000 depending on color tier, edge profile, and slab count. Premium colors (Eternal Calacatta, Ethereal series) sit at the top of the range. Free written quote on the spot during the in-home estimate.
How does Silestone compare to Cambria?
Silestone and Cambria are both premium engineered quartz brands. Comparable performance — non-porous, scratch-resistant, no sealing required. Comparable price tier. The practical difference for Phoenix homeowners working with us: Silestone is broadly distributed and Cosentino lets us serve our customers without the gatekeeping that pushed us away from carrying Cambria. Read our full open letter for the longer story.
What is Dekton and is it better than Silestone?
Dekton is Cosentino's ultra-compact surface — manufactured under high heat and pressure, even tougher than Silestone for outdoor use, fireplace surrounds, and cladding. Silestone is the right choice for indoor kitchen and bath countertops. Dekton wins for outdoor kitchens, BBQ surrounds, and any application exposed to UV. We install both.
Where do I see Silestone slabs in person before buying?
Silestone is available at Cosentino's Phoenix center and through our other partner stone yards. We will walk you through the yard, help you narrow your selections, and mark the exact slab going into your kitchen. The slab walk is part of our standard estimate process — no charge.
How long does Silestone installation take?
Typical timeline: 7–10 business days from your free in-home estimate to final installation. Day 1: estimate + slab selection. Day 2–3: digital laser templating. Day 4–8: CNC fabrication in our shop. Day 9–10: install + sealant + final walkthrough. Most installs are completed in a single day on-site.
