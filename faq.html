Installed quartz countertops in Phoenix and the West Valley typically run $55–$95 per square foot, depending on the brand, edge profile, and slab thickness. We give a firm written quote after a free in-home measurement, so the price you sign is the price you pay.

From signed estimate to installed countertops is usually 7–14 business days. Template day takes about an hour. Install day for an average kitchen runs 4–6 hours, including removal of the old tops.

What's the difference between quartz and granite?
Quartz is engineered (about 93% natural quartz, 7% resin and pigment), so it's non-porous, never needs sealing, and has consistent patterns. Granite is 100% natural stone — every slab is unique, and it should be sealed once a year. Both are heat- and scratch-resistant when used normally.
Do you remove the old countertops?
Yes. Demo and disposal of the existing countertops is included in our standard installation. We protect cabinets and floors, haul the debris, and leave the kitchen broom-clean.
How long do quartz countertops last?
With normal household use, quartz countertops last 25+ years. Most major brands we install — Caesarstone, MSI, Silestone, Pental, Vicostone — carry a residential lifetime limited warranty.

What thickness are your countertops?
Standard quartz and granite slabs are 3 cm (about 1¼ inches) — what we install on kitchens. 2 cm (¾ inch) is available for vanities or laminated edge details. Porcelain is available in 6 mm and 12 mm panels for vertical applications.
What edge profiles do you offer?
Standard included edges are eased (square with a slight chamfer) and quarter round. Upgrade edges include bevel, bullnose, half bullnose, ogee, and double waterfall. We'll show you samples on the slab during template so you see exactly what you're getting.
