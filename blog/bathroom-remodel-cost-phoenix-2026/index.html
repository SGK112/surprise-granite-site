Planning a bathroom remodel in Phoenix? Knowing what to budget is the first step toward a successful renovation. Bathroom remodel costs vary widely depending on the size of the room, the materials you choose, and whether you are making layout or plumbing changes. In this guide, we break down real-world pricing for three common project sizes: half bath, guest bath, and master bath. All costs reflect current 2026 pricing in the Phoenix metro area, including Surprise, Scottsdale, Peoria, Glendale, Mesa, Chandler, Gilbert, and Goodyear.

Whether you are updating a powder room for resale value or building your dream master bathroom from scratch, this guide will help you set a realistic budget and understand where your money goes.

Half Bath Remodel: $3,000 - $8,000

A half bath (also called a powder room) has just a toilet and a sink, typically in 20-30 square feet. Because there is no shower or tub, costs are significantly lower than a full bathroom remodel. Half bath updates are one of the best returns on investment because guests see this room most often.

What $3,000 - $5,000 Gets You (Budget)

  • New vanity with sink and faucet (stock or semi-custom)
  • New toilet (standard efficiency)
  • Fresh paint throughout
  • Updated mirror and light fixture
  • Basic flooring update (vinyl plank or ceramic tile)

What $5,000 - $8,000 Gets You (Mid-Range)

  • Custom or floating vanity with stone countertop
  • Wall-mounted toilet or comfort height model
  • Decorative tile accent wall or wainscoting
  • New flooring (porcelain, natural stone, or patterned cement tile)
  • Upgraded lighting (sconces, recessed, or pendant)
  • New baseboards and trim

Half bath remodels rarely require permits unless you are moving plumbing. Most projects take 5-10 working days from demo to completion.

Guest Bathroom Remodel: $8,000 - $15,000

A guest bathroom is a full bathroom with a shower or tub/shower combo. These typically range from 40-70 square feet. The shower or tub is the biggest cost driver in a guest bath remodel, especially if you are doing a tub-to-shower conversion or retiling.

What $8,000 - $11,000 Gets You (Budget to Mid-Range)

  • Retile shower or tub surround with porcelain tile
  • New vanity with quartz or granite countertop
  • New toilet
  • Updated fixtures (faucet, showerhead, towel bars)
  • New flooring (porcelain tile)
  • Fresh paint, new mirror, updated lighting

What $11,000 - $15,000 Gets You (Mid-Range to Premium)

  • Custom tile shower with niche and bench
  • Tub-to-shower conversion with frameless glass
  • Semi-custom or custom vanity with undermount sink
  • Heated flooring (electric radiant mat)
  • Upgraded tile work (accent walls, mosaic niches)
  • Rain showerhead and handheld combo
  • Exhaust fan upgrade with humidity sensor

Guest bath remodels typically take 2-3 weeks. If you are converting a tub to a walk-in shower, plan for the longer end of that range due to plumbing and drain modifications.

Master Bathroom Remodel: $15,000 - $35,000

The master bathroom is where most homeowners invest the most, and for good reason. You use this space every day, and a well-designed master bath adds significant resale value. Master baths typically range from 70-150 square feet and often include both a shower and a tub, double vanities, and upgraded finishes throughout.

What $15,000 - $22,000 Gets You (Mid-Range)

  • Full gut and rebuild of shower with modern tile
  • Double vanity with quartz countertop
  • New tub (freestanding or drop-in replacement)
  • New toilet (comfort height, elongated)
  • Full floor tile replacement
  • Updated lighting, mirrors, and fixtures throughout
  • Fresh paint and new trim

What $22,000 - $35,000 Gets You (Premium)

  • Custom walk-in shower with linear drain, large-format tile, and frameless glass
  • Freestanding soaking tub
  • Custom double vanity with undermount sinks and stone countertop
  • Heated tile flooring
  • Custom tile work (accent walls, shower niches with lighting, decorative patterns)
  • Linen closet or built-in storage
  • Rainfall showerhead, body jets, or steam shower unit
  • Updated electrical (dedicated circuits, GFCI outlets, in-mirror defogger)

Master bath remodels are the most complex residential bathroom projects. Expect 3-6 weeks for a standard renovation and up to 8-10 weeks for luxury builds with custom tile work, heated floors, and high-end fixtures.

What Affects Bathroom Remodel Cost

Understanding the major cost categories helps you make informed tradeoffs and allocate your budget where it matters most.

Tile and Shower Work

Tile is often the single largest material cost in a bathroom remodel. The price difference between basic ceramic ($1-$5/sq ft) and premium natural stone ($15-$40/sq ft) is dramatic. Installation labor also varies: a simple subway tile layout costs less than a herringbone pattern with multiple accent tiles. For a full shower retile, expect to pay $3,000-$8,000 depending on tile choice, pattern complexity, and shower size. Visit our material comparison page to see how different tile options stack up.

Vanity and Countertop

Bathroom vanities range from $300 for a stock 30-inch single vanity to $3,000+ for a custom 60-inch double. The countertop material adds $200-$1,500 depending on the stone and size. Quartz and granite are the most popular choices for bathroom countertops in Phoenix because they resist moisture and heat well. Explore options in our slab marketplace.

Fixtures and Hardware

Faucets, showerheads, towel bars, toilet paper holders, and similar items typically run $500-$2,500 combined. Upgrading to a rain showerhead, touchless faucet, or wall-mounted fixtures costs more but adds a premium feel. Budget fixtures from Glacier Bay or Peerless are $50-$150 each, while mid-range brands like Moen or Delta run $150-$400, and luxury brands like Kohler or Brizo can exceed $500 per fixture.

Labor

Labor typically accounts for 40-60% of a bathroom remodel budget. In the Phoenix area, skilled tile installers charge $6-$12 per square foot for installation. Plumbers charge $85-$150 per hour, and electricians $75-$125 per hour. General contractors typically mark up labor 15-25% to cover project management, scheduling, and warranty.

Plumbing Changes

If you keep your toilet, sink, and shower/tub in the same locations, plumbing costs are minimal (new supply lines and shut-off valves). Moving a toilet, relocating a shower drain, or adding a second sink requires new rough-in plumbing, which can add $1,500-$5,000 to the project depending on the scope of changes and whether you are on a slab foundation (most Phoenix homes are).

Slab Foundation Note: Most Phoenix homes are built on concrete slabs. Moving drain lines in a slab requires cutting and re-pouring concrete, which is significantly more expensive than moving drains in homes with a crawlspace or basement. Keep existing plumbing locations when possible to save thousands.

Arizona-Specific Cost Factors

Remodeling in the Phoenix metro area has some unique advantages and considerations that affect both cost and material choices.

Hard Water

Phoenix has some of the hardest water in the country, measuring 15-25 grains per gallon in most areas. This affects your bathroom remodel in several ways. Glass shower doors show water spots quickly unless treated or squeegeed daily. Light-colored grout stains faster from mineral deposits. Fixtures with chrome or polished nickel finishes show buildup more than brushed or matte finishes. If you do not have a water softener, consider these factors when selecting finishes and discuss low-maintenance options with your contractor.

Heat and Plumbing

Arizona's extreme heat affects the hot water supply line. During summer months, the "cold" water coming into your home can reach 90+ degrees. This rarely affects the remodel itself, but it is worth knowing when selecting fixtures. Thermostatic shower valves with scald protection are recommended and required by Arizona building code for new construction and remodels.

Dry Climate Advantages

Arizona's low humidity is a genuine advantage for bathroom remodels. Mold and mildew are far less common here than in humid climates. Adhesives, grout, and paint cure faster and more consistently. You can also get away with simpler ventilation solutions than would be required in a humid climate, though a properly vented exhaust fan is still code-required and recommended.

Year-Round Building Season

Unlike regions with harsh winters, bathroom remodels in Phoenix can happen any month of the year. There are no freeze risks for fresh grout or adhesives, and material deliveries are not delayed by snow or ice. The busiest season for remodeling in Phoenix is October through April when the weather is mild. Summer months (June-August) are slightly slower, which sometimes means faster scheduling and occasional discounts.

Permit Costs

In most Phoenix metro cities, a bathroom remodel permit costs $150-$400. Permits are required when you are doing plumbing or electrical work (which is nearly every bathroom remodel). The City of Surprise, Scottsdale, and Peoria typically process permits within 3-5 business days. Factor in 1-2 inspections during the project: one for rough plumbing/electrical and one for the final.

Financing Your Bathroom Remodel

Not every homeowner wants to pay cash for a bathroom remodel, and you should not have to. Here are the most common financing options Phoenix homeowners use:

Home Improvement Loans

Unsecured personal loans specifically for home improvement typically range from $5,000-$50,000 with fixed rates of 6-12% APR and terms of 3-7 years. They do not require home equity and are approved quickly, but rates are higher than secured options. Companies like LightStream, SoFi, and local credit unions offer competitive rates.

Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)

If you have equity in your home, a HELOC offers lower interest rates (typically 7-9% APR in 2026) and flexible borrowing. You only pay interest on what you draw. HELOCs work well for larger master bath projects where costs may fluctuate. The downside is that your home is collateral, and approval takes 2-4 weeks.

Contractor Financing

Many remodeling companies, including Surprise Granite, partner with financing providers to offer payment plans directly. Common options include 12-month same-as-cash (0% interest if paid in full within 12 months) and low-interest plans with 24-60 month terms. This is often the most convenient option because it is handled during the estimating process.

Cash-Out Refinance

For larger projects ($30,000+), some homeowners refinance their mortgage and pull out cash. This works best when current mortgage rates are close to what you are already paying. With 2026 rates in the 6-7% range, this makes sense primarily for homeowners who locked in higher rates in previous years.

How to Save Money on Your Bathroom Remodel

You can have a beautiful bathroom without blowing your budget. Here are the most effective ways to reduce costs without sacrificing quality:

  • Keep the existing layout. Moving plumbing is the single biggest cost escalator. If your toilet, vanity, and shower are in functional locations, renovate in place. You can completely change the look of a bathroom without moving a single drain line.
  • Choose porcelain tile over natural stone. High-quality porcelain that looks like marble or travertine costs $4-$10 per square foot versus $15-$40 for the real thing. Modern porcelain is so realistic that most people cannot tell the difference.
  • Use accent tile strategically. Instead of premium tile everywhere, use it in high-visibility areas: the shower niche, a feature wall behind the vanity, or a decorative floor border. Pair it with complementary but less expensive field tile for the rest.
  • Buy during sales. Tile and fixture sales happen throughout the year. Floor & Decor, Arizona Tile, and our slab marketplace frequently have promotions. Buying tile a few weeks early can save 15-30%.
  • Skip heated floors in small spaces. Radiant floor heating is a luxury feature that adds $800-$1,500 to a bathroom remodel. In a half bath or small guest bath, it is a nice-to-have rather than a must-have.
  • Consider refinishing the tub. If your existing tub is in good structural condition, professional refinishing ($400-$700) gives it a like-new surface at a fraction of replacement cost.
  • Do your own demo. Removing an old vanity, pulling up floor tile, and stripping a shower to the studs is hard work but not technically difficult. Handling demo yourself can save $500-$1,500 in labor.
Money-Saving Tip: The biggest leverage point in any bathroom remodel is the shower tile choice and the layout. A beautiful shower with $5/sq ft porcelain tile in a herringbone pattern will look more expensive than cheap tile randomly paired with an expensive vanity. Spend your visual budget on the largest surface area first.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a bathroom remodel cost in Phoenix in 2026?

Bathroom remodel costs in Phoenix range from $3,000-$8,000 for a half bath, $8,000-$15,000 for a guest bath, and $15,000-$35,000 for a master bath. The final price depends on materials, fixtures, layout changes, and whether you move plumbing. Luxury master bath remodels can exceed $50,000.

What is the most expensive part of a bathroom remodel?

Labor is typically the most expensive component, accounting for 40-60% of the total cost. After that, tile work and the shower or tub area are the biggest material expenses. Moving plumbing or electrical adds significant cost, especially on slab foundations common in Phoenix homes.

How long does a bathroom remodel take in Arizona?

A half bath takes 1-2 weeks of construction, a guest bathroom 2-3 weeks, and a full master bath remodel 3-6 weeks. Add 1-4 weeks for design, material ordering, and permits. Arizona's dry climate and year-round building season mean fewer weather-related delays compared to other states.

Does a bathroom remodel increase home value in Phoenix?

Yes. A mid-range bathroom remodel recoups approximately 60-70% of its cost at resale, according to national remodeling data. In Phoenix's competitive housing market, updated bathrooms are one of the top features buyers look for, particularly in master suites.

Can I finance a bathroom remodel in Phoenix?

Yes. Common financing options include personal home improvement loans, HELOCs, contractor-offered payment plans, and cash-out refinancing. Many Phoenix homeowners use 12-24 month same-as-cash financing for projects under $25,000. Ask your contractor about available financing during the estimate process.
