~8 miles from our shop · 1980s–1990s homes, slightly larger floor plans

Flooring: What Works in 115° Heat

Three categories handle the desert:

  • Tile (24x48 porcelain) — Cool underfoot in summer, scratch-proof, dog-safe, pool-water tolerant. The dominant choice for main living areas in every West Valley city.
  • Engineered hardwood + premium LVP — Solid hardwood is risky in AZ humidity swings (sub-15% in summer, 60%+ monsoon). Engineered hardwood stays stable; LVP is waterproof for kitchens, baths, kids' rooms, and rentals.
  • Natural stone — Travertine for indoor/outdoor continuity, slate for non-slip pool decks, honed marble for primary entries and baths. Annual sealing required for travertine and limestone.

Counters: What Most West Valley Buyers Choose

  • Quartz — The most-installed counter in the West Valley. Zero maintenance, never seal, consistent across slabs. Best for high-traffic kitchens and rentals.
  • Granite — Natural stone, unique patterns, heat-resistant. Ideal when you want one-of-a-kind character. Our slab yard has 200+ to walk.
  • Quartzite — Granite-tough, marble-pretty. The Vistancia and Blackstone favorite. $5,500–$11,000 for an average kitchen.
  • Marble — For baths and low-traffic kitchens. Honed (not polished) for floors. Carrara, Calacatta, Statuary varieties.
  • Bolder Image (Cambria alternative) — Arizona-fabricated quartz at a friendlier price point. We position it as the Cambria alternative for buyers comparing brands.

Cabinets: Reface vs Replace

The decision usually breaks down by home age:

  • Pre-1990 homes (most Sun City) — Boxes are typically solid, refacing wins. $4,500–$9,000 vs $20,000+ for replacement.
  • 1990s–2010s homes (Westwing Mountain, Trilogy, Garden Lakes, PebbleCreek) — Refacing usually wins unless you want a layout change.
  • Newer builder-grade homes (Estrella, Canyon Trails, Vistancia Village) — Often worth replacing because builder cabinets were cost-cut. Custom cabinets unlock the full ceiling height (10–11') that builder boxes leave gaps under.
  • Luxury / historic homes (Litchfield Park, Wigwam, Old Town Peoria) — Custom millwork to match original details. Our in-house shop handles period-correct moldings and panel profiles.

HOA Realities

The major West Valley HOAs we have templated paperwork for:

  • RCSC (Sun City Recreation Centers) — Architectural review for exterior work and certain interior changes.
  • PORA (Property Owners and Residents Association, Sun City West) — Similar process to RCSC.
  • Vistancia Community Association (ARC) — Master-plan covering Vistancia, Trilogy, Blackstone, the Village, Mountainside.
  • Estrella Mountain Ranch — Newland Communities ARC.
  • PebbleCreek — Robson Communities review.
  • Russell Ranch / Dreaming Summit — Litchfield Park luxury communities.
