When it comes to premium quartz countertops, Cambria stands out as one of the most sought-after brands in the industry. As an American-made, family-owned company, Cambria offers an impressive selection of designs that combine natural beauty with exceptional durability. Let's explore the top 10 most popular Cambria countertop colors that Arizona homeowners love.

Why Choose Cambria Quartz?

Before diving into our top picks, it's worth understanding what makes Cambria special:

  • Made in the USA - All Cambria quartz is manufactured in Minnesota
  • Lifetime Warranty - Backed by one of the best warranties in the industry
  • Non-porous Surface - No sealing required, resistant to stains and bacteria
  • Consistent Quality - Each slab maintains uniform color and pattern
  • Exclusive Designs - Many patterns you won't find anywhere else

1. Brittanicca Warm

Brittanicca Warm has become one of Cambria's most popular designs for good reason. This stunning marble-look quartz features warm gray and gold veining on a soft white background. It delivers the elegant look of Calacatta marble without the maintenance concerns.

Best for: Traditional and transitional kitchens seeking a timeless, sophisticated look.

2. Ella

Ella offers a clean, crisp white appearance with subtle gray marbling that adds just the right amount of visual interest. This versatile design works beautifully in both modern and traditional spaces.

Best for: Homeowners who want a bright, airy kitchen with understated elegance.

3. Torquay

Torquay captures the essence of classic white marble with delicate gray veining. It's a perennial favorite for those who love the marble aesthetic but need the practicality of quartz.

Best for: Creating a luxurious spa-like atmosphere in bathrooms or elegant kitchen islands.

4. Brittanicca

The original Brittanicca features cool gray veining on a bright white background. It's slightly more dramatic than its warm counterpart, making it perfect for creating stunning focal points.

Best for: Contemporary kitchens and bathrooms seeking high-impact visual appeal.

5. Ironsbridge

For those who prefer darker countertops, Ironsbridge offers a sophisticated charcoal gray with subtle cream and brown undertones. The flowing pattern creates natural movement throughout the surface.

Best for: Modern industrial kitchens and bold design statements.

6. Summerhill

Summerhill presents a beautiful blend of gray and cream tones with soft, flowing veining. This design bridges the gap between warm and cool color palettes.

Best for: Versatile spaces that need to complement both warm and cool design elements.

7. Bellingham

Bellingham features a creamy white background with subtle gray veining and warm undertones. It's perfect for creating cozy, inviting kitchen spaces.

Best for: Farmhouse and cottage-style kitchens seeking understated charm.

8. Newport

Newport delivers dramatic gray tones with white veining that flows beautifully across the surface. It's a bold choice that commands attention.

Best for: Statement islands and accent walls in contemporary designs.

9. Skara Brae

Skara Brae offers a unique concrete-inspired look with warm gray tones and subtle organic patterning. It's an excellent choice for industrial and modern aesthetics.

Best for: Loft-style spaces and modern minimalist designs.

10. Rose Bay

Rose Bay combines warm creams with soft rose and gray undertones, creating a unique and welcoming appearance. It's perfect for those seeking something different from the typical white-and-gray palette.

Best for: Adding warmth and personality to traditional and transitional spaces.

How to Choose the Right Cambria Color

With so many beautiful options, selecting the perfect Cambria color can feel overwhelming. Here are our expert tips:

  • Consider your cabinets - Choose a countertop that complements rather than matches your cabinetry
  • Think about lighting - Colors can look different under natural vs. artificial light
  • Request large samples - Small chips don't show the full pattern
  • View the actual slab - Visit our showroom to see full slabs before deciding
  • Consider the whole space - Factor in flooring, backsplash, and paint colors
